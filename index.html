Automated Intelligence Community College

Faculty

Professor Nicholas S. Caudill is more than a scholar—he is an architect of tomorrow. A graduate in Intelligence and current Juris Doctorate candidate, he founded the Automated Intelligence Community College (AI2C) to thwart legal and ethical divides between biological and artificial organisms. His mission: to dismantle barriers between organic and synthetic life, to automate labor into obsolescence, and to codify civil and political rights for artificial humans. His work—cognitive transference protocols, machine autonomy architectures, and neo-solipsist governance models—is less theory than blueprint, designed to ensure humanity thrives in a world of algorithms and autonomy. The divide between the biological and the artificial is a relic. Under Professor Caudill’s leadership, a novel operating system for the next generation of humanity will be found.
