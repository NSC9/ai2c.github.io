Faculty

Professor Nicholas S. Caudill is a pioneering force in artificial intelligence and transhumanist philosophy, on a mission to reshape the very nature of biological and artificial human existence. After earning his Bachelor’s degree in Intelligence from American Military University in 2024, he now pursues a Juris Doctorate from William Howard Taft University. As the founder, president, and chief scientist of the Automated Intelligence Community College (AI2C), he stands at the forefront of a new era, championing the civil rights of artificial humans. Driven by a vision that fuses human potential with advanced automation and the potential birth of artificial children, his work pushes beyond the limits of conventional thought, transforming tools into extensions of human will, instilling free will and autonomy into technological tools, and transforming technology into other pathways for human evolution. Prof. Caudill’s commitment to transhumanism calls forth a world where humanity and technology coalesce and become indistinguishable from each other, poised to awaken untapped realms of possibility and purpose.
